On November 1st, 2025, we successfully hosted **System Override CTF 2025** at Asia Pacific University (APU), bringing together over 300 participants from 10+ universities across Malaysia. As one of the lead organizers and CTO of BarqSec, this event represents months of planning, coordination, and teamwork.

## The Vision

When we started planning System Override CTF, our goal was simple: create a world-class cybersecurity competition that would challenge students and foster a community of security enthusiasts in Malaysia. We wanted to provide a platform where students could test their skills, learn from each other, and connect with the broader cybersecurity community.

## Challenges and Categories

The competition featured challenges across multiple categories:

- **Reverse Engineering**: Analyzing and understanding compiled code
- **Cryptography**: Breaking encryption and solving cipher challenges
- **Web Security**: Exploiting web application vulnerabilities
- **Forensics**: Investigating digital evidence
- **Binary Exploitation**: Finding and exploiting software vulnerabilities

## Lessons Learned

Organizing a competition of this scale taught us valuable lessons:

### Technical Leadership
As CTO, I was responsible for:
- Overseeing challenge deployment infrastructure
- Ensuring platform stability during the competition
- Coordinating with Exploit3rs on challenge integration
- Managing technical issues in real-time

### Team Collaboration
- Clear communication is essential when coordinating large events
- Delegation and trust in team members leads to better outcomes
- Having backup plans for technical issues is crucial

### Community Building
- Events like this strengthen the cybersecurity community
- Students are eager for opportunities to test their skills
- Cross-university collaboration benefits everyone

## Impact

The success of System Override CTF 2025 demonstrates:

1. **Student capability**: Malaysian students are ready to compete at high levels
2. **Industry interest**: International sponsors see value in supporting local talent
3. **Community growth**: Events like this create lasting connections
4. **Educational value**: Hands-on competitions complement traditional education

## Looking Forward

This is just the beginning for BarqSec and System Override CTF. We're already planning future events and workshops to continue supporting the cybersecurity community in Malaysia.

If you're a student interested in cybersecurity, I encourage you to:
